# Understanding Diabetes Risk: A Comprehensive Analysis of Demographics, Lifestyle, and Clinical Biomarkers

Diabetes is a growing global health challenge, projected to affect one in eight adults by 2050. 

This study examined demographic, lifestyle, and clinical predictors of diabetes among individuals with and without the condition. 

Age emerged as the strongest demographic factor, while BMI and family history also contributed to risk. Lifestyle behaviours such as low physical activity, poor diet, and inadequate sleep were consistently linked to elevated risk, though screen time was a weak predictor. HbA1c proved the most powerful biomarker, with a threshold around 6.5% marking high probability of diabetes. Protective factors included physical activity, diet quality, and HDL cholesterol.
